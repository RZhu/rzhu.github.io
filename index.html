About

I am an Assistant Professor of Supply Chain and Operations Management at the Purdue Krannert School of Management. Previously, I received my Interdisciplinary Ph.D. in Statistics from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology and B.Eng. degrees in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science from both the Shanghai Jiao Tong University and the University of Michigan in 2015. I spent the summers of 2020 and 2019 as a research scientist intern with Amazon and Google Research.

My research seeks to address fundamental and practical challenges in recommendation systems, digital experimentation, retailing & pricing, and business analytics by connecting tools from machine learning, optimization, and economics. As part of it, I have been collaborating with companies across different industries, such as e-commerce, consumer packaged goods and manufacturing. My works have been recognized by an Honorable Mention in INFORMS George E. Nicholson 2019 Student Paper Competition , a Finalist in POMS-JD.com 2019 Best Data-Driven Research Paper Competition, and a Finalist in INFORMS Service Science 2021 Best Cluster Paper Award.
